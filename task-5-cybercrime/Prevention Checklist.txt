🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Checklist for College Students (India)

✅ General Online Safety

-Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
-Avoid clicking unknown links in emails, SMS, or social media messages—verify the sender first.
-Keep your devices updated with the latest software and security patches.
-Limit personal information shared online, especially on public profiles.
-Download apps only from trusted sources like official app stores (Google Play Store / Apple App Store).
-Log out from shared/public computers and avoid saving passwords on them.

💰Financial & UPI Safety

-Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone—even if they claim to be from the bank.
-Always verify payment requests carefully—remember, you receive money without entering a PIN, but sending money requires a PIN.
-Check UPI IDs and QR codes before paying to ensure they belong to the correct person or business.

⚠️Smart Online Behavior

-Be cautious of “too good to be true” offers, such as fake internships, discounts, or giveaways.
-Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions or sensitive logins.
-Regularly review account activity (banking, email, social media) for any suspicious activity.

🚨If Something Goes Wrong

-Report cyber fraud immediately at the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
-Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** for urgent financial fraud assistance
